<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  <b>Julie Guthman</b> of environmental studies was featured in a <i>Los Angeles Times</i> trend story about the meaning of the term "organic" and changing farming standards.
</p>
<p>
  Literature professor <b>Nathaniel Mackey</b> was quoted in a <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> article about jazz musician Cecil Taylor and the opening of a new documentary film titled <i>Cecil Taylor: All the Notes</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Douglas Lin</b>, professor of astronomy and astrophysics, was quoted in a <i>Space.com</i> story about extrasolar planets.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Brent Haddad</b> of environmental studies coauthored an opinion piece for the <i>Monterey County Herald</i>, calling on officials to develop a plan to control polluted urban runoff that flows into the bay.
</p>
<p>
  Physicist <b>David Smith</b>'s research on gamma ray emissions associated with thunderstorms was featured in a story on <i>PhysOrg.com</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>News &amp; Observer</i> and the <i>Herald-Sun</i> in Durham, North Carolina, wrote about the new mentoring program for beginning teachers that will be administered in partnership with the <b>New Teacher Center</b>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Men's Health</i> magazine ran a blurb about research by psychology's <b>Eileen Zurbriggen</b>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Los Angeles Times</i> reviewed a concert of the <b>UCSC Pacific Rim Music Festival</b> that celebrated the 70th birthday of composer Terry Riley and featured a major new work by the Kronos Quartet, noting the "sheer level of bliss" produced by the three-and-a-half-hour performance.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Capital Times</i> in Madison, Wis., suggested that Marquette University should consider renaming themselves the <b>Banana Slugs</b> since the nickname they've chosen is proving unpopular (<a href="http://www.madison.com/tct/news/index.php?ntid=38915&amp;ntpid=1">see story</a>).<br>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> ran a feature on Eisa Davis, the playwright niece of history of consciousness professor <b>Angela Davis</b>, noting that Eisa's play <i>Angela's Mixtape</i> is a sketch of a life in the shadow of her aunt, "a woman many consider one of the 20th century's most notable American figures."
</p>
