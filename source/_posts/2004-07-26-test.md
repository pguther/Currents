<p class="pagehead">
  UCSC in the News
</p>
<p>
  The <em>New York Times</em> cited assistant dean of the Arts <strong>Keith Muscutt</strong> in an article about Peru's past, noting his authorship of a book about the Chachapoya people titled <em>Warriors of the Clouds</em> and his excavation of mummies dating to 1100 from cliffside mausoleums on a remote Peruvian lake, documented by the History Channel.
</p>
<p>
  Associate professor of Art History <strong>Carolyn Dean</strong> was quoted in a <em>Seattle Times</em> article about the transition of Our Lady of Guadalupe from a sacred image in Mexico to a U.S. pop-culture icon.
</p>
<p>
  <em>New African</em> published a story about history of consciousness professor <strong>Angela Davis</strong> accepting the Honorary Citizen of Paris award on behalf of imprisoned former Black Panther, civil rights activist, and journalist Mumia Abu-Jamal.
</p>
<p>
  The <em>Santa Cruz Sentinel, Monterey County Herald</em> and Salinas <em>Californian</em> announced that music lecturer <strong>Robert Klevan</strong> has been tapped to be the new education director for the Monterey Jazz Festival.
</p>
<p>
  The <em>Albuquerque Journal</em> quoted folk singer Rosalie Sorrels about the donation of her archives to UCSC's <strong>Special Collections</strong>.
</p>American studies professor <strong>Tricia Rose</strong> was quoted in a <em>Boston Globe</em> article about the impact of pop culture on female fashion choices.
