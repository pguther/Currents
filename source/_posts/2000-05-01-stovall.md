History professor Tyler Stovall will present a talk titled "The History of African American Expatriates in Paris." The talk, which is free and open to the public, takes place on Thursday, May 11, from 7 to 8 p.m. at the McPherson Center for Art and History, 705 Front St. A reception follows. Stovall, who is also provost of Stevenson College, studies 20th-century France. He is the author of _The Rise of the Paris Red Belt_ (1990) and _Paris Noir: African Americans in the City of Light_ (1996). The talk is presented as part of the Humanities Lecture Series, sponsored jointly by the Humanities Division and the Art Museum of Santa Cruz County. The series runs through June on the second Thursday of each month. For more information, call (831) 459-2696 or 429-1964.
