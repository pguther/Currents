<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  The 18th annual spring <a href="http://bike2work.com/s_cruz/index.html">Bike to Work Week</a> is May 15-22. There are many new activities this year, including a Rail and Trail Day May 22, a Giant BMX show May 15 at the Bicycle Trip, and a Slug Bike Festival on May 19, Bike to Work Day. See <a href="http://bike2work.com/s_cruz/week.html">full schedule</a> of events.
</p>
<p>
  <img align="right" height="259" src="../art/bike_poster.200.jpg" width="200" alt="">The Slug Bike Festival will take place at Quarry Plaza (Bay Tree Plaza) from 10:30 a.m. to 2:30 p.m. and feature tips on road safety and maintenance, information tables from local on- and off-campus bicycle organizations, music, and a raffle. There will be opportunities to license your bike and find buddies to improve your commute.
</p>
<p>
  Also on May 19, Bike to Work Day, from 6:30 to 9:30 a.m. there will be <a href="http://bike2work.com/s_cruz/breakfast.html">free breakfasts</a> for those who bicycle to work or school at places all over the county, including three sites on campus: the Women's Center, "top of bike path" (sponsored by cycling team), and Quarry Plaza (sponsored by the Bike Co-op).
</p>
<p>
  The Slug Bike Festival is sponsored by Bike 2 Work/Ecology Action, Beckmann's Old World Bakery, Spokesman, Another Bike Shop, Sprockets, UCSC Bike Co-op, UCSC OPERS, and UCSC TAPS. Come help support cycling as a viable transportation alternative. It's good for your health, the environment, and UCSC.
</p>
<p>
  For more information, contact Bonnie McPike at (831) 459-1446 or <a href="mailto:bmcpike@ucsc.edu">bmcpike@ucsc.edu</a>.<br>
</p>
