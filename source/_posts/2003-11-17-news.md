<p class="pagehead">
  UCSC in the News
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Los Angeles Times Book Review</i> called the new book <i>Changing the Powers that Be</i> by psychology's Bill Domhoff "a startlingly fresh analysis" by an author who has "a bracingly firm grasp on the realities of the political world."
</p>
<p>
  Writing in <i>The Nation</i>, columnist Patricia Williams offered high praise for <i>Whitewashing Race: The Myth of a Color-Blind Society</i>, authored by a team of seven that included <b>David Wellman</b> of community studies and <b>Michael Brown</b> of politics. Williams called the book "a definitive treatise" and an "extraordinarily in-depth study."
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Sacramento Bee</i> featured an interview with American studies professor <b>Tricia Rose</b> about her new book <i>Longing To Tell.</i> The <i>San Diego Union-Tribune</i> also ran a profile of Rose that was recently published in the <i>New York Times.</i>
</p>
<p>
  Sociologist <b>Hiroshi Fukurai</b>'s op-ed about juror diversity appeared in the <i>San Francisco Chronicle.</i>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Manuel Pastor</b> of Latin American and Latino studies was cited in a <i>Los Angeles Times</i> article about the challenges facing Latinos trying to achieve the American Dream in Los Angeles. Pastor wrote the summary of the United Way report.
</p>
