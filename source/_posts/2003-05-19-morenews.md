  <span class="sectionhead"><a name="posters" id="posters"></a>Undergraduate researchers to present their work on May 29</span><br>
</p>
<p>
  Undergraduate researchers from the Division of Physical and Biological Sciences and the Baskin School of Engineering will present their findings in the sixth annual Undergraduate Research Poster Symposium on Thursday, May 29.<br>
</p>
<p>
  The event will take place from 3 to 5 p.m. in the Thimann Courtyard (between Thimann Labs and Thimann Lecture Hall).<br>
</p>
<p>
  "There has been an overwhelming response this year, with over 60 posters and 70 student presenters expected at the symposium," said Marlene Robinson, curriculum and outreach coordinator for the Division of Physical and Biological Sciences.
</p>
<p>
  "This is a reflection of the outstanding research opportunities that our undergraduates are engaged in, as well as the excellence in mentoring from our faculty," Robinson said.<span class="sectionhead"><br></span><a href="#reminder"><i>back to top</i></a><br>

<p>
  <span class="sectionhead"><a name="hitchcock" id="hitchcock"></a>Lecturer Emeritus George Hitchcock to read from his new book</span><br>
</p>
<p>
  George P. Hitchcock, lecturer emeritus in creative writing and literature, will read from his new book, <i>One Man Boat: A George Hitchcock Reader,</i> on May 27 at 6 p.m. in the McHenry Library foyer. Hitchcock is the former editor of <i>kayak,</i> a literary journal that has influenced two generations of American writers. His new book is an eclectic collection of poems, plays, short stories, candid interviews, reviews, and essays, that also includes his testimony before the House Un-American Activities Committee, reprinted from the <i>Congressional Record.</i><br>
  <br>
  Hitchcock will be joined at the event by one of his former UCSC students, Jaime Jacinto, who will read from his own <i>Heaven is Just Another Country.</i>
</p>
<p>
  A reception will follow in the library foyer. This event is free and open to the public. For more information, contact Laura Ellis at (831) 459-2167.<br>

</p>
<p>
  The Recreation Department is offering a class in making sensational sushi on May 31 from noon to 3 p.m. in the OPERS Conference Room, East Field House.
