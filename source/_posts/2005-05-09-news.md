<p>
  The media were on hand when research associates <b>Glenn Stewart</b> and <b>Brian Latta</b> banded four peregrine falcon chicks in a nest on the PG&amp;E building in San Francisco. Coverage included the <i>San Francisco Chronicle, Contra Costa Times, Santa Cruz Sentinel</i>, Associated Press, and KPIX TV.
</p>
<p>
  Bombay's <i>Indian Express</i> quoted associate professor of history <b>Dilip Basu</b> in an article about his role in arranging a screening of Indian director Satyajit Ray's film <i>Pather Panchali</i> at the 2005 Cannes Film Festival.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran a story about whether Santa Cruz County beaches are shrinking, with quotes from geologists <b>Gerald Weber</b> and <b>Gary Griggs</b>.
</p>
<p>
  Geophysicist <b>Steven Ward</b> was quoted in a story about tsunami warning systems in <i>American Scientist</i> magazine.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> covered the election of professor emeritus of environmental studies <b>Michael Soule</b> to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
</p>
<p>
  Research on climate change by <b>Lisa Sloan</b>, professor of Earth sciences, was mentioned in a long article on the environment in <i>Congressional Quarterly Weekly</i>.
</p>
<p>
  A study on the effects of ocean warming on elephant seals by <b>Burney Le Boeuf,</b> professor emeritus of ecology and evolutionary biology, was covered on various web sites, including <i>LiveScience, Innovations Report, ScienceBlog, SciScoop</i>, and <i>WildlifeNews</i>.
</p>
<p>
  Professor emeritus of economics <b>Richard Musgrave</b> was mentioned in a (Worcester, Mass.) <i>Sunday Telegram</i> article about economist Attiat Ott of Clark University, who described Musgrave as her true mentor.
</p>
