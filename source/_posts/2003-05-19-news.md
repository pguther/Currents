<p class="pagehead">
  UCSC in the News
</p>
<p>
  Assistant professor of philosophy <b>Alva Noe</b> was the featured guest on a half-hour radio program for Australia's Radio National, discussing his recent article, "Is the Visual World a Grand Illusion?"
</p>
<p>
  <b>Daniel Costa,</b> professor of ecology and evolutionary biology, was featured in a <i>Boston Globe</i> article about the Tagging of Pacific Pelagics (TOPP) program, an ambitious project to equip ocean-going animals with electronic tags to gather detailed oceanographic data and document the animals' migratory habits. Costa oversees the seabird and marine mammal components of TOPP, which includes research on 20 species of whales, seals, birds, sharks, tunas, sea turtles, and large squid.<br>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> reported on a Fulbright-Hays research grant awarded to <b>Elisabeth Cameron,</b> assistant professor of art history, that will enable her to study African culture in Zambia.<br>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Garth Illingworth,</b> professor of astronomy and astrophysics, was quoted in a <i>Washington Times</i> story about how the temporary grounding of the space shuttle fleet is affecting previously scheduled maintenance of the Hubble Space Telescope.<br>
</p>
<p>
  UCSC tennis coach <b>Bob Hansen</b> and the program he has built were profiled in the sports section of the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> prior to the NCAA Division III national championships at Gustavus Adolphus College in Minnesota.
</p>
<p>
  UCSC's number one ranking in space sciences in a recent survey of top universities received print and broadcast news coverage. <b>Stan Woosley,</b> professor and chair of astronomy and astrophysics, was quoted in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel,</i> and <b>Burt Jones,</b> professor of astronomy and astrophysics and assistant director of Lick Observatory, was interviewed by KION TV.<br>
</p>
