<p>
  <b>Gary Glatzmaier</b> and <b>Robert Coe</b>, both professors of Earth sciences, were featured in "Magnetic Storm," a program in the PBS television series <i>NOVA</i> about Earth's magnetic field.
</p>
<p>
  Writing lecturer <strong>Conn Hallinan</strong> contributed a piece to <i>CounterPunch</i> about U.S. foreign policy toward Venezuela.
</p>
<p>
  Environmental studies chair <strong>Daniel Press</strong> was featured in a <i>Sacramento Bee</i> article about the phrase "open space" and the varied definitions it carries.
</p>
<p>
  Environmental toxicologist <b>Donald Smith</b> was quoted in an article in <i>USA Today</i> about lead poisoning in California condors.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Post-Standard</i> of Syracuse ran a story about a speech delivered by history of consciousness professor <strong>Angela Davis</strong> at an anti-war rally in New York.
</p>
<p>
  Astronomer <strong>Garth Illingworth</strong> was quoted in a story about the oldest galaxies in the <i>Spokane Spokesman-Review</i>.
</p>
<p>
  UCSC's <strong>Banana Slug mascot</strong> got top billing in an MSN.com article on college mascots which posed the question, "What's a banana slug and why does it cheer for the University of California, Santa Cruz?"<br>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Mid-County Post</i> ran a story about a new book on the Santa Cruz coast by geologist <b>Gary Griggs</b>.<br>
  <br>
</p>
