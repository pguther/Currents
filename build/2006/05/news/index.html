29 May 2006

UCSC in the News

Public Information Department

Bill Domhoff, research professor of psychology and sociology, featured prominently in a major story in U.S. News & World Report about dreams. The article referred to Domhoff's database of 16,000 dreams that facilitates his work.

Ellen Moir, director of the New Teacher Center, was featured in an article in the Times Educational Supplement about her visit to Scotland, where she was invited to brief leaders of education about the center's cutting-edge work in teacher training and leadership.

In a Sunday "Datebook" article about summer Shakespeare festivals. the San Francisco Chronicle noted that Shakespeare Santa Cruz will be presenting King Lear.

The Santa Cruz Sentinel announced a gift of $75,000 from a consortium of donors to fund Hindi/Urdu language classes at UCSC, initiated by Silicon Valley entrepreneurs Kamil and Talat Hasan.

Media Highlights provides monthly summaries of "UCSC in the News" columns.
