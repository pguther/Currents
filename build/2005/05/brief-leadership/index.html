23 May 2005

UCSC staff attend leadership institute

Public Information Department

UCSC sent 14 staff members to the first UC Leadership Institute, held May 15-17 in San Diego. The program, sponsored by the Office of the President, was designed to help equip UC managers with the skills and knowledge to be effective managers and leaders within the UC system.

The program highlighted many of UC's senior leaders as keynote speakers, including Professor Martin M. Chemers, who spoke on "Heavy Mettle: The Role of Confidence and Optimism in Leadership Effectiveness." The program agenda and presentations are available at the following web site: www.ucop.edu/ucli

UCSC attendees were Michael Barnes, of Admissions; Deana Slater, Kent Bailey, Margaret Collins, and Michael Yamauchi-Gleason, of CUHS; Buddy Morris, of EH&S; John Hammond and Patrick LeCuyer, of ITS; Lai-Ying Hsiung, of the University Library; Peter McMillan, of BAS; Jesse Magana, of Physical Plant; Ronaldo Ramirez and Jim Burns, of University Relations; and Rafael Granados, of UCCP

The attendees were selected by a campus committee that included Lisa Rose, Karen Eckert, Mark Cianca, Marie Logan, Jean Marie Scott, Ciel Benedetto, and Barbara Perman.
