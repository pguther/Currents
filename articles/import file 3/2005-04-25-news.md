<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  The <i>Chronicle of Higher Education</i> ran a story about the new Engineering 2 Building in its campus architecture supplement, with a quote from <b>Peyman Milanfar</b>, associate professor of electrical engineering.
</p>
<p>
  Research by biologist <b>Don Croll</b> on the effects of foxes introduced to the Aleutian Islands was covered in the <i>Christian Science Monitor, Miami Herald</i>, and <i>Duluth News Tribune</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Black Enterprise</i> reported the results of economist <b>Rob Fairlie</b>'s research for the Small Business Administration on black self-employment rates.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Laser Focus World</i> ran a story about <b>Roberto Manduchi</b>, assistant professor of computer engineering, and his work on a laser-based assistive device for the blind.
</p>
<p>
  A story in <i>New Scientist</i> magazine on the use of femtosecond lasers by cell biologists includes the work of a team led by <b>Yishi Jin</b>, professor of molecular, cell, and developmental biology.
</p>
<p>
  Geophysicist <b>Steven Ward</b> was quoted in another article about tsunamis, this time in <i>Science World</i> magazine.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Herald-Sun</i> in Durham, North Carolina, wrote about the school district's partnership with the <b>New Teacher Center</b> to provide mentors for novice teachers.
</p>
<p>
  Physicist <b>Hartmut Sadrozinski</b> was quoted in a story in the <i>San Luis Obispo Tribune</i> about a weather balloon project for high school students organized by the Santa Cruz Institute for Particle Physics.
</p>
<p>
  <b>William Berry</b>, managing director of the University Affiliated Research Center at NASA Ames, was quoted in a <i>San Jose Business Journal</i> story about the NASA Ames Research Center.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Asbury Park Press</i> (New Jersey) ran a feature on history of consciousness professor <b>Angela Davis</b> prior to her appearance at Rutgers University to speak on the role of American women in transforming culture, history, and politics. And <i>Metro Santa Cruz</i> ran a story about jazz singer Billie Holiday, quoting from Davis's book, <i>Blues Legacies and Black Feminism: Gertrude "Ma" Rainey, Bessie Smith, and Billie Holiday.</i>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Michael Riordan</b>, adjunct professor of physics, was quoted in a <i>New York Times</i> story about Moore's Law.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran a story about biologist <b>Melissa Jurica</b>, who won a prestigious Searle Scholarship.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran a story about the peregrine falcons released at Long Marine Lab, with quotes from <b>Glenn Stewart</b> of the Predatory Bird Research Group.
</p>
<p>
  Research by environmental toxicologist <b>Roberto Gwiazda</b> on the impact of household lead hazard control measures on blood lead levels in children was reported in <i>Health &amp; Medicine Week, Hematology Week, Life Science Weekly, Science Letter, Lab &amp; Health Weekly, Lab Law Weekly</i>, and other newsletters.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Metro Santa Cruz</i> published a story about music lecturer <b>Mesut Ozgen</b>'s use of classical Turkish melodies in his original compositions for classical guitar.
</p>
