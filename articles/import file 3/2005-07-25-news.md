<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> published an opinion piece coauthored by Chancellor <b>Denice D. Denton</b> and Intel chairman Craig Barrett about the new initiative to increase the number and quality of math and science teachers in California.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> did a feature story about the apprenticeship in organic farming and gardening offered by the Center for Agroecology &amp; Sustainable Food Systems.
</p>
<p>
  A team of engineering students got some help on their senior project from SanDisk engineers, who rescued data from saltwater-soaked memory cards, and their story got widespread media coverage, including the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> and online news sites of <i>USA Today, Chicago Tribune, Los Angeles Times, Dallas Morning News, Baltimore Sun, Newsday, Forbes, Morningstar,</i> CNet News, and more. <b>John Vesecky</b>, professor of electrical engineering, and student <b>Roberto Menchaca</b> were quoted in the stories.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Rob Fairlie</b>'s work on black self-employment was featured in <i>Black Enterprise</i>, and Fairlie was also interviewed by <i>Inc.</i> magazine about his research on access to technology and entrepreneurship. The <i>Long Island Business News</i> also wrote about that research.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran a big front page story about the continued relevance of an environmental report issued 35 years ago by UCSC students led by <b>Gary Griggs</b>, now director of the Institute of Marine Sciences. In addition, Griggs and <b>Jonathan Zehr</b>, professor of ocean sciences, were quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> story about a major grant from the Moore Foundation for a marine microbiology lab.
</p>
<p>
  Professor Emeritus <b>Ronald Grieson</b> coauthored an opinion piece about California's redistricting process that appeared in the <i>Riverside Press-Enterprise.</i>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Paul Ortiz</b>'s book tour for <i>Emancipation Betrayed</i>, about black resistance to white supremacy in Florida, has included interviews on KPFA and KUSP Radio and Ohio NPR affiliate WOSU.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Brent Haddad</b> was interviewed by KSCO Radio about the new statewide desalination study he's overseeing.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Fortune</i> magazine cited work by economist <b>Lori Kletzer</b> in an article about job retraining and government programs.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Financial Times</i> reported that economist <b>Mike Dooley</b> is being considered as a Federal Reserve governor.
</p>
