<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  With the return of two Britons who'd been held in solitary confinement at Guantanamo Bay for up to 18 months, <i>BBC News</i> relied on psychology's <b>Craig Haney</b> for an extensive report on the effects of such deprivation.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Denver Post</i> ran an article about research by <b>Lisa Sloan</b>, professor of Earth sciences, and graduate student <b>Jacob Sewall</b> on the effects of climate change on the Rocky Mountain snowpack.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Los Angeles Times</i> ran a feature about alumnus <b>Kent Nagano</b> and his work on the symphonic work, <i>Manzanar: An American Story</i>, describing a preview of its world premiere that recently took place at UCSC's <b>Pacific Rim Festival</b>.
</p>
<p>
  Biologists <b>Terrie Williams</b> and <b>Jim Estes</b> were featured in a story on NPR's <i>All Things Considered</i> about their research on the effects of killer whale predation on sea otter populations in Alaska.
</p>
<p>
  The Associated Press picked up the story out of Anchorage about Alaska's mentor-teacher program, which was modeled after the work of the <b>New Teacher Center</b>.
</p>
<p>
  Literature professor <b>Nathaniel Mackey</b> was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> story about being selected to present UCSC's annual faculty research lecture next year.
</p>
<p>
  Biologist <b>Giacomo Bernardi</b> was quoted in a <i>Los Angeles Times</i> article about grunion, the only fish that leave the water to spawn on beaches.
</p>
<p>
  Assistant professor of film and digital media <b>L. S. Kim</b> was quoted in <i>ABC News</i> online about stereotypes of Asian actors in Hollywood.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> lauded the Theater Arts Department's "spectacularly bright and cheerful production" of Sondheim's musical, <i>Merrily We Roll Along</i>, noting director (and department chair) <b>Danny Scheie</b>'s "mischievous eye for irony" and "brilliant eye for flamboyant drama."
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran a story about <b>Robert Kraft</b>, professor emeritus of astronomy and astrophysics and former director of UCO/Lick Observatory, who has been honored by the Astronomical Society of the Pacific.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran an article about an upcoming seminar for high school teachers and the annual weeklong Dickens Universe, to be held this summer as part of the 25th anniversary of UCSC's <b>Dickens Project</b>.
</p>
<p>
  An article on <i>Space.com</i> about a new theory of the formation of the solar system included a quote from <b>Douglas Lin</b>, professor of astronomy and astrophysics.
</p>
<p>
  Sesnon Gallery director <b>Shelby Graham</b> was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> story about local art galleries.
</p>
