<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Economist <b>Mike Dooley</b> continues to garner media attention for his views regarding foreign investment in the U.S. economy, most recently in <i>Newsweek</i>'s cover story about the falling dollar.
</p>
<p>
  Research by biologist <b>Donald Croll</b> and his collaborators on the effects of introduced foxes on birds and vegetation in the Aleutian Islands was the subject of stories in the <i>New York Times, Washington Post, Newsday</i>, and online news sites <i>Live Science</i> and <i>Science Daily</i>.
</p>
<p>
  Sociologist <b>Hiroshi Fukurai</b> was quoted in a <i>San Francisco Daily Journal</i> article about charges that anti-Semitism influenced the makeup of jury panels in capital cases in Alameda County from the mid-1980s to mid-1990s. The <i>New York Times</i> picked up the story, bringing national attention to claims of bias in the juror selection process, which is Fukurai's specialty.
</p>
<p>
  Art professor <b>Dee Hibbert-Jones</b> was quoted in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> about a public art project in collaboration with Santa Cruz County elementary schools.
</p>
<p>
  A story in <i>Science News</i> magazine focused on the planet formation theories of <b>Douglas Lin,</b> professor of astronomy and astrophysics. Another story in <i>Science News</i> about new observations of extrasolar planets included quotes from astronomer <b>Greg Laughlin</b>.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Rob Fairlie</b>'s research on Asian entrepreneurs was covered by <i>Business Week</i> and KQED Radio, and then Fairlie fielded a call from <i>Money</i> magazine for an article on earnings growth among entrepreneurs.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Ira Pohl</b>, professor and chair of computer science, was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> story about a new track in computer game design for computer science majors.
</p>
<p>
  Sociologist <b>Mike Males</b> was widely quoted in a <i>Family Practice News</i> story about claims that a reported drop in drug use among teens is linked to a public-service ad campaign.
</p>
<p>
  <i>InsideBayArea.com</i> ran a story about an exhibition of paintings by art professor emerita <b>Kathryn Metz</b> at the Wiegand Gallery in Belmont.
</p>
<p>
  A story in the <i>Ann Arbor News</i> about a public lecture by <b>Paul Koch</b>, professor of Earth sciences, discussed Koch's ideas about the extinction of mastodons and other large animals in North America during the late Pleistocene.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Renee Tajima-Pena</b> of community studies was quoted in a <i>San Francisco Bay Guardian</i> writeup of the film <i>a.k.a.</i> <i>Don Bonus</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Berkshires.com</i> announced that American studies professor <b>George Lipsitz</b> will be the keynote speaker at a conference to examine the future of ethnic studies at Williams College.
</p>
