<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  An Associated Press story deconstructing the withdrawal of Supreme Court nominee Harriet Miers quoted politics professor <b>Dan Wirls</b>.
</p>
<p>
  Research on the source of lead contamination in chocolate by environmental toxicologists <b>Charley Rankin</b>, <b>Russell Flega</b>l, and others was covered in <i>Science News</i> magazine and the <i>Chicago Tribune</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Register-Pajaronian</i> covered a visit by Harvard physicist Lisa Randall to Watsonville High School, sponsored by Arts &amp; Lectures, to help celebrate the investiture of <b>Chancellor Denton</b>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Time</i> magazine interviewed a number of people on campus for its story about the nationwide student movement to help preserve family farming by serving locally grown food in campus dining halls.
</p>
<p>
  An article in <i>New Scientist</i> magazine about a NASA proposal for how to deflect an Earth-bound asteroid included a quote from <b>Erik Asphaug</b>, associate professor of Earth sciences.
</p>
<p>
  National Public Radio interviewed film expert <b>B. Ruby Rich</b> of community studies about the new Palestinian movie <i>Paradise Now</i>, which explores the motives of suicide bombers.
</p>
<p>
  Assistant professor of theater arts <b>Patty Gallagher</b> was quoted in a <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> article about a new Cirque du Soleil show that pays homage to clowns.
</p>
<p>
  Oregon's <i>Register-Guard</i> noted that soprano Maria Jette tracked down UCSC music lecturer <b>Irene Herrmann</b>, the executrix of author/composer Paul Bowles's musical estate, in search of rare material to perform in concert.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Register-Pajaronian</i> ran a story on the $195,452 grant awarded to professor of English and comparative literature <b>Murray Baumgarten</b> from the National Endowment for the Humanities to fund a summer institute for college and university teachers in Venice, Italy.<br>
</p>
