<p class="pagehead">
  UCSC in the News
</p>
<p>
  Articles in the <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> and the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> about coastal erosion and sea walls included quotes from <b>Gary Griggs,</b> professor of Earth sciences. Griggs was also quoted in a <i>Sentinel</i> story about a new web site that offers information about the Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary.<br>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> reviewed a new book of essays by history of consciousness professor <b>Donna Haraway</b> titled <i>The Haraway Reader,</i> calling her "one of the most eloquent writers about the love-hate relationship between humans and machines you will ever read.''
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> and the Watsonville <i>Register-Pajaronian</i> ran stories about the naming of one of the original research buildings at Long Marine Laboratory in honor of <b>William Doyle,</b> professor emeritus of biology and founder of the Institute of Marine Sciences.<br>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Peter Raimondi,</b> professor of ecology and evolutionary biology, was quoted in an article in the <i>Portland Oregonian</i> about a new study indicating that sea urchins can live 100 years or more.
</p>
<p>
  Music professor <b>Fred Lieberman</b> was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Metro</i> article about the multiphonic singing of Tibet's Gyuto monks.<br>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Bradford Holden,</b> a postdoctoral researcher in astronomy and astrophysics, was quoted in news stories about his team's investigation of a galaxy considered the biggest, brightest, hottest, and one of the most ancient star-forming regions yet viewed in the universe. Stories appeared in the <i>Everett Washington Herald, Honolulu Star, Honolulu Advertiser, China Post,</i> and the <i>Independent</i> (South Africa).
</p>
