<p class="pagehead">
  UCSC in the News
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Chronicle of Higher Education</i> cited several passages from history professor <b>Mark Cioc</b>'s book <i>The Rhine: An Eco-Biography, 1815-2000</i> in an article titled "The Power of Water."<br>
</p>
<p>
  An article in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> about a pair of golden eagles frequenting the East Field meadow included quotes by <b>Janet Linthicum</b> of the Predatory Bird Research Group.
</p>
<p>
  Film and digital media professor and department chair <b>Chip Lord</b> contributed a piece to the <i>Bay Guardian</i> about last year's visit by film star Nicolas Cage to a UCSC film class.<br>
</p>
<p>
  History professor <b>Dana Frank</b> appeared on CNN's <i>Lou Dobbbs Tonight</i> program in a segment titled "Whatever Happened to Made in America?" Frank also spoke about job outsourcing on San Francisco's KRON.<br>
</p>
<p>
  Art professor and department chair <b>Norman Locks</b> was quoted in a <i>Register Pajaronian</i> article about a Watsonville art show that featured several of his photographs.<br>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Salinas Californian</i> ran an article about folksinger Rosalie Sorrels, noting that "UCSC has set up her archive "in recognition of her contributions to American culture in the second half of the twentieth century."
</p>
