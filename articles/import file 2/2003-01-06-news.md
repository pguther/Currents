<p class="pagehead">
  UCSC in the News
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Los Angeles Times</i> tapped <b>Brent Haddad</b> of environmental studies for comment on a high-stakes move by the Bush administration to resolve a long-standing water dispute between Imperial Irrigation District and coastal Southern California.
</p>
<p>
  Psychology's <b>Bruce Bridgeman</b> was quoted in the <i>Cincinnati Enquirer</i> about proposals to vaccinate children against smallpox, which he said "borders on the criminal."
</p>
<p>
  The UCSC <b>New Teacher Center</b> was the model for a teacher mentor project in Madison, Wisconsin, that was written up in the <i>Capital Times.</i>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Anthony Pratkanis</b> of psychology began the year with a high-profile appearance on NPR's <i>Talk of the Nation</i> program, discussing international propaganda and the role of the United States in the world. That followed a December interview on KNRC Radio in Denver about war propaganda.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Michael Hutchison</b>'s recent media appearances included a KSBW-TV interview about the local economy that focused on the glut of available office and retail space in downtown Santa Cruz.
</p>
<p>
  Research at an early Indian pueblo in New Mexico by anthropology's <b>Diane Gifford-Gonzalez</b> was featured in a story in <i>American Archaeology,</i> the quarterly publication of the Archaeological Conservancy.
</p>
