<p>
  A 15-part BBC television series called "California Bay," focusing on marine life and research around Monterey Bay, featured several UCSC marine scientists, including biologists <b>Terrie Williams</b> and <b>Baldo Marinovic,</b> geologist <b>Gary Griggs,</b> head dolphin trainer <b>Billy Hurley,</b> and many of the student volunteers who help care for resident dolphins <b>Primo</b> and <b>Puka</b> at Long Marine Laboratory.<br>
  <br>
  The <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> ran a story about astrophysicist <b>Stan Woosley's</b> theory regarding the origins of gamma ray bursts.<br>
  <br>
  Earth sciences researcher <b>Erik Asphaug's</b> research on asteroid impacts was included in an article about asteroids in the <i>New York Times.</i><br>
  <br>
  The work of associate professor of mathematics <b>Richard Montgomery</b> was featured in the magazines <i>Science News</i> and <i>Science.</i> Montgomery's novel solution of the "three-body problem"--the 300-year-old conundrum of understanding how three planets or stars move under each other's mutual gravitational attraction--showed that the orbits of a trio of stars bound by gravity could trace a figure-eight pattern through space.
</p>
<p>
  Athletic director <b>Mark Majeski</b> was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> article about the triumph of the Banana Slug nickname over all other competitors--including the South Dakota School of Mines "Hardrockers"--in the <a href="http://www.sportsuniversity.com/special_features/poll_gallery/index.shtml">SportsUniversity.com</a> National Funniest Nickname Championship.
</p>
