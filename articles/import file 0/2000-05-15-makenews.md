<p>
  History of consciousness's <b>Angela Davis</b> coauthored an in-depth article on prison conditions for the Opinion pages of the <i>Sunday Examiner &amp; Chronicle.</i> Her piece focused on security housing units--cells that put prisoners in solitary confinement--and was coupled with another by C.A. "Cal" Terhune, director of the California Department of Corrections.
</p>
<p>
  For a review of the IMAX movie <i>Dolphins,</i> the <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> interviewed <b>Alejandro Acevedo-Gutierrez,</b> a postdoctoral researcher in the Institute of Marine Sciences whose work with dusky dolphins in Patagonia was featured in the film.<br>
  <br>
  Professor of earth sciences <b>Robert Anderson</b> and graduate student <b>Lesley<br>
  Perg</b> were featured in a pair of articles in the <i>Salinas Californian</i> about Perg's research on the rate of uplift of the marine terraces along the Santa Cruz coast.
</p>
<p>
  <b>College Nine</b> made a media splash last week, receiving coverage on KCBS and KLIV radio, in the <i>San Jose Mercury News,</i> on Bay City News Service, and on KSBW-Channel 8.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> recently ran a story about librarian <b>Margaret Gordon</b>, noting that her name will soon be known by millions of romance novel fans thanks to a dedication in the <i>New York Times</i> bestseller novel <i>The Wicked Widow.</i>
</p>
<p>
  "Tomfoolery," a musical review of more than 20 of mathematician <b>Tom Lehrer's</b> satirical songs, is playing in Walnut Creek and getting rave reviews from critics at regional papers, including the <i>San Francisco Chronicle.</i> At the same time, a new three-CD box set titled <i>The Remains of Tom Lehrer</i> is due out this month. That release, which features such all time favorites as "Poisoning Pigeons," was featured in the <i>Santa Cruz CountySentinel.</i>
</p>
