<p>
  An article in <a href="http://pubs.acs.org/email/cen/html/053100174848.html"><i>Chemical and Engineering News</i></a> featured the research of professor of chemistry and biochemistry <b>Glenn Millhauser.</b> Millhauser and his coworkers are using a technique called electron spin resonance to study protein structures. <b><br>
  <br>
  Karl Gebhardt,</b> a Hubble postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Astronomy and Astrophysics, was quoted in an article in the <i>New York Times</i> about research on supermassive black holes in the centers of galaxies conducted by Gebhardt and an international team of collaborators.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Patricia Zavella</b> of community studies was quoted in an article in the <i>Arizona Republic</i> on machismo and Latino culture. The article was reprinted in the <i>Mexico City News.</i>
</p>
<p>
  Research astronomer and operations manager at Lick Observatory <b>Remington Stone</b> appeared on KNAX's <i>Good Morning Sacramento,</i> the top-rated morning television show in the Sacramento area. The show did a live broadcast from Lick Observatory in May, and Stone served as the gracious host.
</p>
<p>
  A final project in an art class taught by <b>Nobuho Nagasawa</b> turned into a memorable <i>Good Times</i> cover June 8. The cover story, about Y2K graduates, was illustrated by an image of five students walking off into a grassy meadow, carrying tool boxes and wearing short-sleeved blue workshirts. The props were part of the students' final project, for which they each designed individual artworks to give to their classmates and Nagasawa. The toolboxes were a gift from Nagasawa. The students pictured were <b>Peter Hokama, Virgina Kleker, Sean Horchy, Amy Bunker, and Holly Chang Gummelt.</b><br>
  <br>
  A front-page story in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> on the Center for Adaptive Optics featured <b>Jerry Nelson,</b> professor of astronomy and astrophysics and director of the center, <b>Sandra Faber,</b> University Professor of astronomy and astrophysics, and <b>Anthony Misch,</b> a specialist at Lick Observatory.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Elaine Wheeler</b> of UC's online college prep program was interviewed by reporters from <i>wired.com</i> and <i>Crosstalk.</i><br>
