<p>
  Psychology's emeriti have been out in force lately. <b>Elliot Aronson</b> penned an op-ed on school violence for the <i>Los Angeles Times</i> following the Michigan tragedy. And his colleague <b>Tom Pettigrew</b> was tapped by the <i>Times,</i> as well as the <i>San Francisco Examiner,</i> for comment on John McCain's use of a derogatory term to refer to his captors in Vietnam.
</p>
<p>
  Continuing their quest for planets outside the solar system, professor of astronomy and astrophysics <b>Steven Vogt</b> and his colleagues recently discovered the smallest extrasolar planets yet detected, although these Saturn-size planets are still much larger than Earth. Vogt was interviewed by local TV stations KION-Channel 46 and KSBW-Channel 35 and was quoted in the <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel.</i> His role in the findings was also mentioned in articles in the <i>New York Times, San Francisco Chronicle, San Jose Mercury News,</i> and in an Associated Press wire story.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> sought the expertise of <b>Manuel Pastor</b> for a story on changes among the Latino electorate. <b>Pastor</b> also coauthored a commentary for the <i>Los Angeles Times</i> about pollution risks faced by minorities in southern California.
</p>
<p>
  <b>The Farm</b> was a lovely backdrop for a recent CNN story about Aptos organic foods maven Nell Newman.
</p>
<p>
  Russia's recent presidential election kept political scientist <b>Michael Urban</b> busy fielding media calls from the BBC, as well as KPFK Radio in Los Angeles.<br>
