<p>
  Literature's <b>Tyrus Miller</b> was quoted extensively in a story in the <i>Chronicle of Higher Education</i> on how teachers are broadening the canon of modernist writers they use in the classroom. The quotes were drawn from Miller's book, <i>Late Modernism: Politics, Fiction, and the Arts Between the World Wars</i> (UC Press, 1999).
</p>
<p>
  Planetary scientist <b>Erik Asphaug</b> was quoted in stories about asteroids and planet formation that ran in the <i>Dallas Morning News</i> and other newspapers. Asphaug wrote a commentary accompanying a research article in the journal <i>Nature,</i> and his colorful language was widely quoted. (The asteroid Mathilde, Asphaug wrote, "looks as though some deity has taken large bites from a cosmic potato.")
</p>
<p>
  When French lecturer <b>Miriam Ellis</b> was awarded the rare honor of knighthood from the French government, the news was picked up by local television and newspapers. Covering the story were KSBW-Channel 8, KION-Channel 46; the <i>Watsonville Register-Pajaronian,</i> and the <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel.</i> Ellis received the award, the Palmes Academique, in recognition of her outstanding work in promoting French arts and culture.
</p>
<p>
  Psychology lecturer <b>Don Saposnek</b> was quoted in a recent <i>Washington Post</i> article on divorce mediation, discussing the value of utilizing the principles of aikido in conflict resolution and the issues involved in the regulation of mediation nationally.
</p>
