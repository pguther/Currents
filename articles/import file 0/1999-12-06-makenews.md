<p>
  The discovery of six new planets outside the solar system by astronomer <b>Steven Vogt</b> and his coworkers received widespread media coverage. <i>The New York Times, USA Today, Boston Globe, San Francisco Chronicle, San Jose Mercury News,</i> and <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> were among the newspapers that covered the story. Broadcast media coverage included KION TV (Ch. 46) and KGO and KCBS radio. Online coverage included Discovery Channel Online and Yahoo! News.<br>
  <br>
  Astronomer <b>Jerry Nelson</b> was featured in a long article in the <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> about how astronomers are contemplating the construction of bigger, more powerful telescopes.<br>
  <br>
  ScienceNOW, the online news service of <i>Science</i> magazine, ran a story about the asteroid impact research of geophysicist <b>Steven Ward</b> and planetary scientist <b>Erik Asphaug.</b> Ward and Asphaug analyzed the potential for an asteroid impact in the ocean to generate a destructive tsunami.<br>
  <br>
  Professor of computer science <b>David Haussler</b> was quoted in an article about computational genomics in the medical journal <i>Annals of Internal Medicine.</i>
</p>
<p>
  <b>Bill Domhoff</b> of psychology was quoted in a lengthy <i>New York Times</i> story about new clues to why we dream.
</p>
<p>
  Choreographer and dance-injury specialist <b>Ruth Solomon</b> was quoted in an AP story on how dancers can train to avoid the back injuries that plague the profession. The story was picked up by a number of papers, including <i>USA Today</i> and the <i>Chicago Daily Herald.</i><br>
  <br>
  A story in the <i>Salinas Californian</i> about the lessons learned from the Loma Prieta earthquake featured seismologist <b>Karen McNally</b>.<br>
  <br>
  The Coastal Conservancy's magazine <i>California Coast &amp; Ocean</i> featured several UCSC scientists in an issue focusing on California's wild islands. <b>Stephen Gliessman</b>, professor of environmental studies, and <b>Brian Walton</b>, coordinator of the Santa Cruz Predatory Bird Research Group, were both quoted in articles, and UCSC graduate student <b>Josh Donlan</b> and alumnus <b>Bradford Keitt</b> authored an article about their work to control feral cats on islands in Baja California.<br>
