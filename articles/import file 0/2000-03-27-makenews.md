<p>
  <br>
  In looking back over a year of theater, <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> theater critic Steven Winn named the <b>Shakespeare Santa Cruz</b> production of <i>Arms and the Man</i> as one of the best ten productions in the region for 1999. The play was directed by theater arts professor <b>Paul Whitworth</b> who, Winn said, "confidently mastered (George Bernard) Shaw's satiric study of military muddlers with one of the region's most sparkling classic productions of the year."<br>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> ran an item about UCSC's role in NASA's Gamma ray Large Area Space Telescope, mentioning astronomer <b>Stephen Thorsett</b> and the Santa Cruz Institute for Particle Physics (SCIPP).
</p>
<p>
  The <b>UCSC Orchestra and Chamber Singers</b>, conductor <b>Nicole Paiement</b>, and pianist <b>Maria Ezerova</b> all received a glowing review from the music critic at the <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> following a recent performance. The critic wrote that they proved "classical music is flourishing on 'the hill.'" The critic went on to describe Ezerova as "amazingly powerful" with "fire and flair."
</p>
<p>
  In a story on a forthcoming California Agricultural Workers History Center in Watsonville, the <i>Santa Cruz County Sentinel</i> named <b>Geoffrey Dunn</b> of community studies as one of two local historians who helped envision and solidify the concept for the project, which was approved unanimously by the Watsonville City Council.<br>
