<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Research by economist <b>Michael Hutchison</b> and <b>Ilan Neuberger</b> (Ph.D. international economics, '00) was featured in a <i>New York Times</i> article about developing nations that guard against financial crises by accumulating large foreign currency reserves.
</p>
<p>
  A comment about prison conditions from psychology's <b>Craig Haney</b> in the <i>Los Angeles Times</i> prompted a letter to the editor from a reader.
</p>
<p>
  National Public Radio picked up news about a report that addresses the "second disaster" in the wake of Hurricane Katrina, including environmental justice issues. The report was coauthored by <b>Manuel Pastor</b> of Latin American and Latino studies and issued by the Russell Sage Foundation.
</p>
<p>
  Astrophysicist <b>Stan Woosley</b> was quoted in an article about gamma-ray bursts on <i>Space.com</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Indian Life &amp; Style</i>, a leading Indian American magazine distributed in the United States and India, published an extensive feature story on assistant professor of music <b>Dard Neuman</b> and his role as UCSC's first-ever endowed chairholder in classical Indian music.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Bill Domhoff</b> of psychology and sociology was quoted about the Bohemian Grove in a recent <i>New Yorker</i> article.
</p>
<p>
  An article in <i>Science News</i> about timekeeping standards included quotes from <b>Steven Allen</b>, a programmer/analyst for UCO/Lick Observatory.
</p>
<p>
  Retired University Librarian <b>Allan Dyson</b> was quoted in obituaries for photographer Philip Hyde in the <i>Philadelphia Inquirer, Monterey County Herald</i>, Wyoming <i>Tribune-Eagle</i>, Salt Lake City's <i>Deseret Morning News</i>, Indiana's <i>Fort Wayne Journal-Gazette</i>, and South Carolina's <i>The State</i>. (Hyde's archive resides in UCSC's Special Collections.)
</p>
<p>
  The Sunday "Datebook" section of the <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> ran a photo of a mixed-media painting by art lecturer <b>Don Fritz</b>, noting it was on exhibit in Mountain View.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Boston Globe</i> quoted emeritus professor of history <b>John Dizikes</b> throughout a story about a local troupe's production of the opera <i>Carmen</i>. (Dizikes is the author of the book <i>Opera in America</i>.)
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Capital Times</i> ran a story on history of consciousness professor <b>Angela Davis</b>'s talk at the University of Wisconsin-Madison where she noted that the U.S. is promoting strategies of incarceration based on torture in Iraq, Afghanistan, and in secret CIA prisons.
</p>

</p>
