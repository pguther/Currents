<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  <b>Pamela Perry</b> of community studies was tapped by <i>USA Today</i> for comment on generational differences in matters of race and ethnicity. The story ran in numerous newspapers, including the <i>Green Bay Press-Gazette</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Erik Asphaug</b>, associate professor of Earth sciences, was quoted in a <i>Christian Science Monito</i>r article about the asteroid Itokawa.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Donald Potts</b>, professor of ecology and evolutionary biology, was interviewed by National Public Radio about the establishment of the Northwestern Hawaiian Islands National Marine Sanctuary.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Emily Brodsky</b>, assistant professor of Earth sciences, was interviewed by KCBS and KSCN radio about her research on earthquake aftershocks, which was also covered by <i>MSNBC.com, LiveScience, Physics Web</i>, and an Associated Press story that ran in the <i>San Jose Mercury News</i>, <i>Washington Post</i>, and other newspapers.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> ran an article on a major donation to UCSC of photographs by acclaimed American photographer Brett Weston valued at more than $1 million, quoting <b>Christine Bunting</b>, head of Special Collections at McHenry Library.
</p>
<p>
  KSBW TV (Channel 8) interviewed <b>Thorne Lay</b>, professor of Earth sciences, about a minor earthquake that was felt in the Santa Cruz area.
</p>
<p>
  Women's studies professor <b>Bettina Aptheker</b> was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> article on the annual Santa Cruz pride rally.
</p>

</p>
