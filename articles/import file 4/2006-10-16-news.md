<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  <b>Michael Mateas</b>, assistant professor of computer science, is prominently featured in a lengthy article about video games in the <i><a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/doc/200610u/rauch-interview">Atlantic Monthly</a></i>.
</p>
<p>
  UCSC Foundation trustee and renowned photographer <b>Frans Lanting</b> is featured on the <a href="http://www.apple.com/pro/">Apple Pro</a> web site. The <a href="http://www.apple.com/pro/profiles/lanting/">profile</a> focuses on Lanting's latest project, "Life: A Journey Through Time," and his conversion midway through the project to all-digital images.
</p>
<p>
  Water expert <b>Brent Haddad</b> of environmental studies appeared on National Public Radio's <i>Science Friday</i> program as part of a discussion of national and international water issues.
</p>
<p>
  An article in <i>Technology Review</i> on efforts to develop cheap, fast genome sequencing features biochemist <b>David Deamer</b>.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Jonathan Fox</b> of Latin American and Latino studies was quoted in a <i>Monterey Herald</i> article about the Mexican government's ban on U.S.-grown lettuce, most of which is produced in California.
</p>
<p>
  <i>American Theatre</i> magazine noted <b>Shakespeare Santa Cruz</b>'s 25th anniversary and described the two local exhibits honoring this milestone.
</p>
<p>
  Biology graduate student <b>Michael Weise</b> discussed his work with sea lions in an episode of the PBS television series <i>Jean-Michel Cousteau Ocean Adventures</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <b>David Haussler</b>, professor of biomolecular engineering, was quoted in a story in <i>New Scientist</i> about comparisons of human and Neanderthal DNA.
</p>
<p>
  <i>New Scientist</i> also ran a feature story on cosmology with quotes from physicist <b>Anthony Aguirre</b>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Richmond Times-Dispatch</i> ran a story about biologist <b>Terrie Williams</b> and her research on the physiology of carnivores.
</p>
<p>
  Seismologist <b>Thorne Lay</b> was quoted in a story on <i>Inside Bay Area</i> about North Korea's test of a nuclear device.
</p>
<p>
  Stories about the Packard Fellowship awarded to astronomer <b>Constance Rockosi</b> ran in Bay City News and the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran a story about a project led by astronomer <b>Gregory Laughlin</b> that invites public participation in the search for new planets.
</p>

</p>
