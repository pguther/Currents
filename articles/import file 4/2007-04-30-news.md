<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Astronomer <strong>Steve Vogt</strong> was quoted in a <i>Chicago Tribune</i> article about the discovery of an extrasolar planet with potentially Earthlike characteristics.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Manuel Pastor</strong> of Latin American and Latino studies penned an opinion piece for the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> about efforts to link development of green industries to greater opportunities for the poor and people of color.
</p>
<p>
  An article in <i>New Scientist</i> magazine about the sequencing of the rhesus macaque genome included a quote from <strong>David Haussler</strong>, professor of biomolecular engineering.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Salinas Californian</i> wrote about the new book by <strong>Ryan Coonerty</strong> of legal studies, who has published a coffee-table book about the nation's monuments.
</p>
<p>
  Dream expert <strong>Veronica Tonay</strong> of psychology was featured in a <i>Sacramento Bee</i> article about bad dreams that got widespread media play through the Scripps Howard News Service, appearing in the <i>Mobile Register</i>, the <i>Monterey Herald</i>, and many other papers.
</p>
<p>
  Astronomer <strong>Francis Nimmo</strong> was featured in a <i>New Scientist</i> story about the effects of an ancient impact on the moon.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> interviewed <strong>Paul Ortiz</strong> of community studies for their coverage of the Reel Work May Day Labor Film Festival. Ortiz directs the annual festival.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Glenn Stewart</strong> and other researchers with the UCSC Predatory Bird Research Group continued to get media attention for their work with nesting peregrine falcons, the latest round including stories in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel, San Jose Mercury News,</i> Associated Press, and the web sites of TV stations KGO and KPIX, while Stewart has also been working with a reporter for National Public Radio and producers for KQED TV and Radio.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Sacramento Bee</i> featured the work of <strong>Paul Frymer</strong> of legal studies in a story about the controversy over remarks by radio celebrity Don Imus.<br>
  <br>
  <i>Space Daily</i> is the latest publication to cover the work of <strong>Chris Wilmers</strong> of environmental studies.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> and <i>Fortune Small Business</i> picked up on a study by economist <strong>Rob Fairlie</strong> about the number of new companies launched in Silicon Valley in the past five years.<br>
</p>

</p>
