<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News, Monterey County Herald,</i> and <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran feature articles on <strong>Shakespeare Santa Cruz</strong> (SSC), quoting managing director <strong>Marcus Cato</strong> and artistic director/theater arts professor <strong>Paul Whitworth</strong>. In reviews of SSC's <i>Endgame</i>, the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> noted that Beckett's absurdist tragicomedy is "enlivened by Whitworth's commanding performance" and Santa Cruz's <i>Good Times</i> said that the play is "arguably the best platform yet for Whitworth's astonishing trove of talent." <i>Metro Santa Cruz</i> also reviewed a performance of SSC's <i>Much Ado About Nothing</i>, noting that the production "strikes the perfect comic note."
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Chronicle of Higher Education</i> is the latest publication to write about professor emeritus <strong>Elliot Aronson's</strong> new book, <i>Mistakes Were Made (But Not By</i> Me).
</p>
<p>
  Campus veterinarian <strong>Dave Casper</strong> was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> story about a gray whale in the Santa Cruz Harbor and in a <i>Napa Register</i> story about harbor porpoises in the Napa River.
</p>
<p>
  Psychology's <strong>Bill Domhoff</strong> fielded a call from <i>Real Simple</i> magazine for an upcoming article about dreams.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> reported on <i>Native Hubs: Culture, Community, and Belonging in Silicon Valley and Beyond</i>," a new book by assistant professor of American studies <strong>Renya K. Ramirez</strong> about how urban Native Americans, who travel back and forth from city to reservation, can strengthen their culture and identity.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Metro Santa Cruz</i> ran a story about electrical engineer <strong>Holger Schmidt</strong>'s research on optoelectronics for biological sensors.
</p>
<p>
  The <strong>New Teacher Center's</strong> partnership with Durham Public Schools in North Carolina was featured in <i>Medill Reports</i>, a publication of Northwestern University.
</p>
<p>
  Computer engineer <strong>Luca de Alfaro</strong> developed a program to color-code Wikipedia entries for reliability that received coverage in the <i>San Francisco Chronicle, Santa Cruz Sentinel, Monterey County Herald, Chronicle of Higher Education,</i> and <i>Dr. Dobb's Journal.</i>
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Don Rothman</strong>, senior writing lecturer and founding director of UCSC's Central California Writing Project, contributed an op-ed to the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> about the value of Shakespeare and the presence of Shakespeare Santa Cruz in the community.
</p>
<p>
  An interview with politics professor <strong>Michael Urban</strong> about how the outcome of the presidential election might impact Russia was reproduced in the press extracts of <i>What the Papers Say Part B</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Grand Rapids Press</i> ran a story about the pervasiveness of pornography that quoted psychology's <strong>Eileen Zurbriggen</strong>.
</p>
<p>
  Assistant dean of the arts <strong>Keith Muscutt</strong> was quoted in a Louisville <i>Courier-Journal</i> story about the discovery by Peruvian archeologists of the earliest documented gunshot victim in the Americas.
</p>
<p>
  New Teacher Center director <strong>Ellen Moir</strong> was featured in an article about Scotland's educational system that appeared in <i>The Times Educational Supplement</i>, Britain's leading education publication.
</p>
<p>
  Biologist <strong>Daniel Costa</strong> was featured in a story in <i>Technology Review</i> magazine about researchers using seals to gather data on the oceans and climate change.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <strong>Rob Fairlie's</strong> work on entrepreneurship was cited in an <i>Indianapolis Business Journal</i> article about the number of young people who want to work for themselves.
</p>

</p>
