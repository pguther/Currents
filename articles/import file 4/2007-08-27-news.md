<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Computer scientist <strong>Luca de Alfaro</strong> continues to make headlines for developing a program that color-codes Wikipedia text to indicate its reliability, with stories appearing in the <i>London Times, Jerusalem Post,</i> the German magazine <i>Spiegel,</i> and the web site Boing Boing.
</p>
<p>
  Physicist <strong>Abe Seiden</strong> was quoted in a <i>Chicago Tribune</i> article about plans for a new particle accelerator at Fermilab.
</p>
<p>
  Biochemist <strong>David Deamer</strong> was quoted in an AP story about artificial life that ran in the <i>Halifax Herald</i> (Canada) and in an article about the origins of life on <i>LiveScience.com</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> and <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran obituaries for <strong>Gurdon Woods</strong>, the sculptor and visionary arts educator who helped establish the UCSC Art Department.
</p>
<p>
  An article about high-tech animal tracking in <i>Cosmos</i> magazine featured biologists <strong>Daniel Costa</strong> and <strong>Scott Shaffer.</strong>
</p>
<p>
  Physicist <strong>Jason Nielsen</strong> was quoted in an article in <i>New Scientist</i> magazine about possible observations of the hypothetical subatomic particle known as the "Higgs boson."
</p>
<p>
  An article in <i>New Scientist</i> about a spectacular supernova included quotes from astrophysicist <strong>Stan Woosley.</strong>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Albuquerque Journal</i> ran a story on a new book by Humanities Division lecturer <strong>Emily Abbink</strong> about the history of Santa Fe's nearly 400-year-old seat of government.
</p>
<p>
  Campus veterinarian <strong>Dave Casper</strong> was quoted in a story in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> and <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> about dead juvenile salmon sharks washing up on local beaches.
</p>
<p>
  Astronomer <strong>Constance Rockosi</strong> spoke to KGO Radio about Google Sky, a new program for astronomy buffs, and was joined by fellow astronomers <strong>Garth Illingworth</strong> and <strong>Xavier Prochaska</strong> to discuss the program on KSBW-TV News.
</p>
<p>
  Postdoctoral researcher <strong>Kena Fox-Dobbs</strong> is featured in a two-part National Geographic TV special, "Prehistoric Predators," about the dire wolf and sabertooth cat.
</p>
<p>
  Assistant professor of theater arts and design <strong>Brandin Baron-Nusbaum</strong> was featured in a <i>Contra Costa Times</i> article about how current fashion trends date back historically hundreds of years.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran a feature and photo spread on Shakespeare Santa Cruz actor and theater arts lecturer <strong>Mike Ryan</strong>, also quoting theater arts professor and SSC artistic director <strong>Paul Whitworth</strong>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Arizona Daily Star</i> noted that assistant theater arts professor <strong>Patty Gallagher</strong>, an expert in Balinese dance, clowning, and Shakespearean drama, will be presenting workshops about the use of masks during her tenure as artist-in-residence for Tucson's Rogue Theater.
</p>

</p>
