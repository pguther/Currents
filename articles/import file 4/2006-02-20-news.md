<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  <b>David Deamer</b>, professor of chemistry and biochemistry, was featured in stories about research on the origins of life in the <i>Economist</i> magazine, <i>London Times, Science Now</i>, and the BBC News web site.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Pamela Perry</b> of community studies was quoted in a <i>USA Today</i> article about the role of race in young relationships, noting developmental differences among high school and college students. The article appeared in numerous other papers, including the <i>Marshfield News</i>.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Rob Fairlie</b>'s study of employer-funded health care mandates, like the recently passed "Wal-Mart Bill" in Maryland, got widespread coverage in publications that included <i>Managed Care Law, Law &amp; Health Weekly, Obesity, Fitness &amp; Wellness Week, Life Science Weekly, Science Letter, Health &amp; Medicine Week,</i> and <i>Managed Care Weekly Digest.</i> Fairlie found that bills that target large businesses fail to make a dent in the number of uninsured.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Turkish Daily News</i> ran a story on the music and career of music lecturer and guitarist <b>Mesut Ozgen</b>.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Gretchen Andreasen</b>, director of the CalTeach Resource Center, was interviewed on radio stations KGO and KCBS about the launch of UCSC's California Teach program.
</p>
<p>
  Theater arts professor and Shakespeare Santa Cruz (SSC) artistic director <b>Paul Whitworth</b> was featured in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> article about SSC's 25th anniversary season.
</p>
<p>
  <b>James Gill</b>, professor of Earth sciences, was featured in a story about his research on undersea volcanoes in the <i>Mid-County Post</i>.
</p>

</p>
