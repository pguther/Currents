<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  The <i>New York Times</i> quoted linguistics professor <b>Geoffrey Pullum</b> in a story about a debate over the nature of language, prompted by a study of starlings that was reported in the current issue of <i>Nature</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Paul Ortiz</b> of community studies did an interview with the Associated Press about the battle between farmworkers and McDonald's over workers' wages.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Craig Agnor</b>, a researcher in the Earth Sciences Department, received considerable media attention for explaining the origins of Saturn's moon Triton, including an interview on KSBW TV and stories in <i>New Scientist</i> magazine, the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel, Lexington Herald-Leader, Register</i> (U.K.), <i>Space.com, Universe Today, Physics Web</i>, and <i>Science Now</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Orin Martin</b> of the Alan Chadwick Garden was featured in a lengthy <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> article about the benefits of planting vertical rows on slopes.
</p>
<p>
  Physicist <b>Michael Dine</b> was quoted in a story about controversial results from a physics experiment in <i>Science</i> magazine.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Sean Swezey</b> of the Center for Agroecology &amp; Sustainable Food Systems was quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> article about a new strategy to control lygus bugs, which ravage strawberries.
</p>
<p>
  Biology student <b>Milara Gatcke</b> was featured in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i>'s "Namedropping" column as the recipient of the $5,000 regional Soroptimist International Women's Opportunity Award.
</p>

</p>
