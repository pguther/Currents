<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Economist <strong>Lori Kletzer</strong> was interviewed by the <i>New York Times</i> about wage insurance.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>David Lackner</strong>, director of the affiliates program for the Bio-Info-Nano R&amp;D Institute (BIN-RDI), was interviewed on KLIV and KSCO Radio and quoted in a <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> story about the institute, which was also covered in the <i>San Francisco Chronicle, Nanotechnology News,</i> Nanotechwire.com, and SiliconValley.com. (See <a href="http://press.ucsc.edu/text.asp?pid=1081">UCSC press release.)</a>
</p>
<p>
  Astronomer <strong>Sandra Faber</strong> and postdoctoral researcher <strong>Susan Kassin</strong> were featured in coverage of the AEGIS galaxy survey on CBC Radio and in <i>USA Today, Science News,</i> UPI, Space Daily, SpaceRef, Space.com, Slashdot, Innovations Report, CCN Magazine, and Earthtimes.org. (See <a href="http://press.ucsc.edu/text.asp?pid=1080">UCSC press release.)</a>
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Rob Fairlie</strong> fielded a call about immigrant women who are entrepreneurs from a reporter with USINFO, a U.S. State Department-run web site about U.S. foreign policy and American life and culture.
</p>
<p>
  Anthropologist <strong>Adrienne Zihlman's</strong> comments about new research that proves that chimpanzees make weapons and kill prey received further distribution, appearing in <i>Science News</i>, the <i>Los Angeles Times</i>, the <i>Scotsman</i>, and as well as in papers in Arizona, Texas, and elsewhere.
</p>
<p>
  A <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> story about red-legged frogs included quotes from biology graduate student <strong>Antonia D'Amore.</strong>
</p>
<p>
  <i>IEEE Spectrum</i> magazine ran a story about astronomer <strong>Gregory Laughlin's</strong> project to involve amateur astronomers in the hunt for new planets.
</p>
<p>
  An article in the <i>Monterey County Weekly</i> about threats to marine mammals on the Central Coast included quotes from biologist <strong>Daniel Costa.</strong>
</p>
<p>
  Producers with Japan's Fuji network contacted <strong>Bruce Bridgeman</strong> of psychology for a feature they're preparing about the Mystery Spot. Bridgeman published a scholarly article about the psychological principles underlying the popular tourist attraction's bizarre effects on perception.
</p>
<p>
  The Santa Cruz <i>Good Times</i> ran an article describing how Pulitzer Prize-winning author Jane Smiley did research for her new Hollywood novel by sitting in on a UCSC class titled <i>Working in TV and Film,</i> taught by alumnus and UC Santa Cruz Foundation trustee <strong>Loren Steck</strong>.
</p>

</p>
