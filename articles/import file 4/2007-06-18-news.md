<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  The <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> quoted associate professor of music <strong>Karlton Hester</strong> for an article about the intersection of jazz, religion, and spirituality.
</p>
<p>
  In an article about grassroots activists' involvement in Democratic Party politics, <i>In These Times</i> cited work by research professor <strong>Bill Domhoff</strong> of psychology and sociology.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>New York Times</i> reviewed a performance by Ben Munisteri Dance Projects of a new work titled <i>Terra Nova</i>, citing the contributions of assistant theater arts professor <strong>Ted Warburton</strong> and Digital Arts and New Media (DANM) alumnus <strong>Timothy Jordan</strong>.
</p>
<p>
  A <i>Seattle Times</i> columnist, fretting about his teenage son becoming a driver, cited reassuring fatality statistics evaluated by sociology instructor <strong>Mike Males</strong>, who "thinks grown-ups load a lot of undeserved baggage onto teens."
</p>
<p>
  A <i>New York Times</i> story about notable commencement speakers around the country included an excerpt from a speech given by history of consciousness professor <strong>Angela Davis</strong> at Grinnell College.
</p>
<p>
  The Associated Press story about our "porn-driven" culture that featured psychology's <strong>Eileen Zurbriggen</strong> continues to make the rounds, appearing most recently in <i>The Star</i> of South Africa and <i>The Pantagraph</i> of Bloomington, Illinois.
</p>
<p>
  The Santa Cruz <i>Good Times</i> ran a story on "Emergence 2007," the year-end public exhibition of the <strong>Digital Arts and New Media (DANM)</strong> graduate program. KSBW-TV also covered the story.
</p>

</p>
