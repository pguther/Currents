<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  June 4 , 2007
</p>
<p>
  Psychologist <strong>Elliot Aronson's</strong> new book got a nice plug in a <i>Newsweek</i> column by Sharon Begley.
</p>
<p>
  Major media outlets, including <i>Money, Forbes, BusinessWeek</i>, the <i>New York Daily News, CNN Money,</i> and the <i>Washington Post</i>, jumped to cover the latest in a series of Kauffman Foundation reports on entrepreneurship by economist <strong>Rob Fairlie</strong>.
</p>
<p>
  Film and digital media professor <strong>Chip Lord</strong> contributed a Top Ten piece to the May 2007 issue of <i>ArtForum</i>.
</p>
<p>
  Marine biologist <strong>Vicki Pearse</strong> was featured in stories in the <i>Columbus Dispatch,</i> UPI wire service, <i>FoxNews.com,</i> and <i>LiveScience.com</i> about a new species of sea anemone she discovered that lives on whale carcasses.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Jim Leap</strong>, manager of the UCSC Farm, was profiled in the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> in a story that highlighted his commitment to sustainable agriculture.
</p>
<p>
  A <i>New Scientist</i> magazine story about a theory that prehistoric inhabitants of North America were wiped out by a catastrophic comet impact included quotes from paleontologist <strong>Paul Koch.</strong>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Des Moines Register</i> ran an article about a commencement address by history of consciousness professor <strong>Angela Davis</strong> at Grinnell College, where she said that today's students need to "blaze new paths" in the fight for social justice.
</p>
<p>
  The Santa Cruz <i>Good Times</i> described an exhibit created by students in assistant art professor <strong>Elliot Anderson</strong>'s <i>Intro to Computer Art</i> class, on display at the downtown Museum of Art and History.
</p><i>Monterey Herald</i><strong>Mesut Ozgen</strong>
