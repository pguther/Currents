<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Economist <strong>Lori Kletzer</strong> was interviewed by <i>The Economist</i> magazine for an article about globalization's "losers."
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Lisa Sloan,</strong> professor of Earth and planetary sciences, was prominently featured in a series of articles on climate change in the <i>Contra Costa Times, San Jose Mercury News,</i> and <i>Vallejo Times Herald.</i>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Chicago Tribune</i> featured linguistics professor <strong>Geoffrey Pullum</strong> in an article about a linguistic urban legend: that Eskimos have countless words for "snow."
</p>
<p>
  Assistant dean of the arts <strong>Keith Muscutt</strong> was featured in the <i>Los Angeles Times</i>, DailyIndia.com, <i>Hindustan Times,</i> and in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> front-page story about the discovery of a previously unknown pre-Columbian ruin in Peru.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Robert Fairlie</strong>, also of economics, appeared in a <i>CNN Money</i> story about business ownership, saying the recent fluctuations of the labor market may have prompted displaced technology professionals to turn to entrepreneurship.
</p>
<p>
  Psychology's <strong>Faye Crosby</strong> was quoted in a <i>Business Week</i> story about mentoring.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Stephen McCabe,</strong> research and education coordinator at the Arboretum, was quoted in an article in the <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> about the effects of freezing temperatures on area gardens.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Gainesville Sun</i> of Florida discussed the work of <strong>Paul Ortiz</strong> of community studies in an article that appeared before Ortiz gave a public talk about his new book <i>Emancipation Betrayed.</i>
</p>
<p>
  The "clue crew" for the TV show <i>Jeopardy</i> spent several hours shooting video at the <strong>Seymour Marine Discovery Center</strong> last week for a program that will air several months from now.
</p>
<p>
  An article in the <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> about the idea of multiple universes includes quotes from cosmologist <strong>Anthony Aguirre.</strong>
</p>
<p>
  Physics graduate student <strong>Jeff Jones</strong> was featured in an article about dark matter in the universe on PhysOrg.com.
</p>
<p>
  Research on raindrop formation by climate scientists <strong>Patrick Chuang</strong> and <strong>Jennifer Small</strong> was featured on LiveScience.com, Monsters &amp; Critics, and UPI newswire.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> reported that UCSC received two gifts valued at $350,000 to create a global cyber cafe and reading garden at <strong>McHenry Library</strong>.
</p>

</p>
