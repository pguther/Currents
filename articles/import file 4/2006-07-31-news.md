<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  <b>Rob Fairlie</b>'s research on entrepreneurship continues to generate media interest, including a recent mention in the <i>New York Times</i>, coverage in the <i>Colorado Springs Business Journal</i>, and questions from ABC News about older workers and entrepreneurship.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Michael Hutchison</b> was pleased to see his new book about Japan's economic downturn during the 1990s featured prominently in an article in the <i>Economist</i> magazine about that Japan's recovery.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>National Journal</i> tapped <b>Jonathan Fox</b> for postelection analysis of the presidential contest in Mexico.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Lori Kletzer</b> was in Washington, D.C., for a Brookings Institution event that got press coverage from U.S. News Wire and other outlets.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Mid-County Post</i> ran an article about seismologist <b>Emily Brodsky</b>'s research on earthquakes and groundwater flow.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Ellen Moir</b> of the New Teacher Center was interviewed by <i>Black America Web</i> for a story about the nationwide shortage of certified teachers.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> wrote about the <b>New Teacher Center</b>'s latest local initiative, working with 24 of the area's best teachers to help them reach the next level of excellence.
</p>
<p>
  Research on ribozymes by <b>William Scott</b>, associate professor of chemistry and biochemistry, and graduate student <b>Monika Martick</b> was reported in <i>Medical Research News</i>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Science and Theology News</i> ran a story about the new book <i>Quantum Enigma</i> by physicists <b>Bruce Rosenblum</b> and <b>Fred Kuttner</b>.
</p>

</p>
