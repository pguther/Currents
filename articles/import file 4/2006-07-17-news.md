<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Providing analysis of the Mexican presidential election, <b>Jonathan Fox</b> of Latin American and Latino studies appeared on <i>The NewsHour with Jim Lehrer</i> and was interviewed by the <i>San Jose Mercury News</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The new major in computer game design (see <a href="games.asp">Currents story</a>) received extensive media coverage--<b>Ira Pohl</b>, professor and chair of computer science, was interviewed by the <i>San Jose Mercury News</i>, <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i>, TV stations KTVU and KION, and radio stations KGO, KLIV, KSCO, and KCSN. Stories also ran on the AP and UPI news wires, in the <i>San Jose Business Journal, Sacramento Business Journal, San Francisco Business Times, Washington (D.C.) Business Journal</i>, and numerous other business journals across the country, as well online news sites such as <i>SiliconValley.com, Monsters and Critics</i>, and <i>GameSpot.</i>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Boston Globe</i> ran a story about <i>Far from the Madding Gerund,</i> a new book by linguistics professor <b>Geoffrey Pullum</b> and Mark Liberman of the University of Pennsylvania, featuring some of the best posts from the blog Language Log.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Brent Haddad</b> of environmental studies discussed desalination on the nationally syndicated radio program <i>A World of Possibilities</i>.
</p>
<p>
  Seismologist <b>Emily Brodsky</b> was quoted in stories about her research on earthquakes and groundwater flow by Scripps Howard News Service, Reuters, <i>ABCNews.com, Space Daily, Hindustan Times</i>, and Xinhua General News Service.
</p>
<p>
  Newhouse News Service interviewed psychology instructor <b>Veronica Tonay</b> for a story about dreams that appeared in papers around the country, including the <i>Post-Standard</i> of Syracuse.
</p>
<p>
  Sociology's <b>Dane Archer</b> was featured in an <i>Albany Times Union</i> article about social norms and blunders in public restrooms.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Rob Fairlie</b> was quoted in the <i>Wilmington News Journa</i>l regarding Delaware's last-place ranking in a recent national review of entrepreneurial spirit around the country that Fairlie conducted for the Ewing Marion Kauffman Foundation. Fairlie also appeared in the <i>Columbus Dispatch</i> in an article about business associations that encourage entrepreneurship among immigrants, noting that such networks have helped fuel the growth in immigrant-owned businesses.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Michael Brown</b> of politics appeared on KUSP Radio's <i>Talk of the Bay</i> to discuss Hoover Institution fellow Shelby Steele's new book, <i>White Guilt: How Blacks and Whites Together Destroyed the Promise of the Civil Rights Era</i>.
</p>
<p>
  Psychology's <b>Anthony Pratkanis</b> was quoted in an Associated Press article about people who rent billboards to deliver messages and make personal appeals. The practice can be expensive but effective, he said.
</p>
<p>
  An article in the <i>Fresno Bee</i> about peregrine falcons included quotes from <b>Glenn Stewart</b> of the Santa Cruz Predatory Bird Research Group.
</p>
<p>
  Geophysicist <b>Steven Ward</b> has been featured in several episodes of the History Channel's Mega Disasters series--"West Coast Tsunami," "East Coast Tsunami," and "Asteroid Apocalypse" (see <a href="brief-disasters.asp">Currents story</a>).
</p>
<p>
  Biologist <b>Daniel Costa</b>'s research on fur seals was covered in the <i>Cape Times</i> (South Africa).
</p>
<p>
  <a href="http://www.ucsc.edu/news_events/media_highlights"><b>Media Highlights</b></a> provides monthly summaries of "UCSC in the News" columns.
</p>
