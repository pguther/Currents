<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Anthropology's <strong>Nate Dominy</strong> rode a tsunami of media coverage of his latest work about the diet of early human ancestors. Heavyweights like <i>The New York Times</i>, <i>Nature</i>, BBC News, National Public Radio, <i>New Scientist</i>, the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation, and United Press International weighed in, as did publications including <i>The Daily Telegraph</i>, the <i>International Herald Tribune,</i> the <i>Times of India</i>, Earthtimes.org, <i>The Scientist</i>, <i>Science Daily</i>, the India edition of <i>zeenews.com, Hindustan Times, Science Now,</i> and Bloomberg.
</p>
<p>
  Humanities dean <strong>Georges Van Den Abbeele</strong> was quoted in <i>The Times of India</i> for an article about a gift of $367,000 to establish an endowed chair in Sikh and Punjabi studies in UCSC's History Department. <i>India-West</i>, the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> and <i>SikhNet News</i> also ran stories on the gift.<br>
  <br>
  <i>The Scientist</i> magazine ran an article about biochemist <b>Roberto Bogomolni's</b> research on light-sensing bacteria.<br>
  <br>
  <i>BusinessWorld</i> featured sociologist <strong>Dane Archer's</strong> research about nonverbal sensitivity in an article on social intelligence.
</p>
<p>
  An Associated Press story about computer scientist <strong>Luca de Alfaro's</strong> Wikipedia project ran in the <i>Columbus Ledger-Inquirer, Fort Worth Star Telegram, Capital,</i> and other newspapers.
</p>
<p>
  <i>BusinessWeek</i> interviewed economist <strong>Rob Fairlie</strong> about his research on home values and entrepreneurship.
</p>
<p>
  Professor of art <strong>Norman Locks</strong> and associate art professor <strong>Lewis Watts</strong> were quoted in <i>Metro Santa Cruz</i> for an article about <i>Chronotopographies: Remembering in Digital</i>, an exhibition of their work at the Felix Kulpa Gallery.
</p>
<p>
  Astronomer <strong>Steven Vogt's</strong> work on the Automated Planet Finder telescope was featured in <i>SpaceRef.com.</i>
</p>
<p>
  While she was in Barcelona, anthropologist <strong>Margaret Gibson</strong> saw her research on the educational experiences of immigrant children in California and Catalonia featured in Spain's <i>El Periodico</i> and <i>La Vanguadia</i> newspapers.
</p>
<p>
  Physicist <strong>Michael Dine</strong> was quoted in a <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> story about the Food Stamp Challenge in which he is participating by spending only $3 a day on food for one week.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Metro Santa Cruz, Good Times</i> and the <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> ran stories on a Santa Cruz appearance and film screening by a pioneer of India's alternative cinema, organized by associate professor of history <strong>Dilip Basu</strong>, director of UCSC's <strong>Satyajit Ray Film and Study Collection</strong>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Science Daily</i> ran a story about UCSC's new Chemical Screening Center directed by chemist <strong>Scott Lokey.</strong>
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> and <i>Good Times</i> reported that <strong>Jeanette Pilak</strong> will become the new director of UCSC's Arts &amp; Lectures program.
</p>

</p>
