<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  Researchers with the UC Santa Cruz Predatory Bird Research Group found themselves in the midst of a media frenzy when they rescued three eggs from a peregrine falcon nest on the San Francisco Bay Bridge--stories featuring researchers <strong>Brian Latta, Glenn Stewart, Janet Linthicum,</strong> and <strong>Brian Walton</strong> ran on the CBS Evening News, KNTV, KPIX, KTVU, and KSBW TV, CNN Radio, <i>San Francisco Chronicle, Santa Cruz Sentinel, Seattle Times, Kansas City Star,</i> AP wire service, and CNN.com.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Brent Haddad</strong> and <strong>Michael Loik</strong> of environmental studies were quoted in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> article about the growing opportunities for consultants to help communities grapple with the potential local impacts of global warming.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Investor's Business Daily</i> and <i>Entrepreneurial Mind</i> were among the papers that wrote about a survey by economist <strong>Robert Fairlie</strong>, which found that Silicon Valley's entrepreneurship rates were higher than the national average during the 1990s and outpaced the rest of the country during the post-boom period.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Ryan Coonerty</strong> of legal studies was the featured guest on National Public Radio's <i>Talk of the Nation</i> program, discussing his new book, <i>Etched in Stone: Enduring Words From Our Nation's Monuments</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Columbus Dispatch</i> ran a story about a speech by history of consciousness professor <strong>Angela Davis</strong> at the National Society of Black Engineers' 33rd annual convention, where she urged members to keep up the fight against racism.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Shakespeare Santa Cruz</strong> was named the top theater troupe and the third best festival in <i>Metro Santa Cruz</i>'s Readers' Choice Goldies Awards. The campus radio station, KZSC-FM, was also named best radio station.
</p>
<p>
  In an article about the enthusiastic response across the country to new politically and socially relevant operas, the <i>Los Angeles Times</i> noted that UCSC would be staging Lou Harrison's <i>Young Caesar</i>. And <i>Metro Santa Cruz</i> ran a story on <i>Young Caesar</i>, the opening event in UCSC's weeklong opera symposium "New Directions in Contemporary Opera."
</p>

</p>
