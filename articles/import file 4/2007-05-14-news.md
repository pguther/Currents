<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  <strong>David Haussler</strong>, professor of biomolecular engineering, was quoted in a story about the opossum genome on NPR's <i>Morning Edition</i>.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Francisco Chronicle</i> covered the recent work of anthropology's <strong>Nate Dominy</strong> and grad student <strong>Justin Yeakel</strong>, who are investigating the diet of early human ancestors.
</p>
<p>
  A theory about multiple universes put forth by physicist <strong>Anthony Aguirre</strong> prompted a story in <i>New Scientist</i> magazine.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>Patricia Zavella</strong> of Latin American and Latino studies gave a lengthy interview in Spanish to TV Azteca, the second-largest Mexican television network, about her research on the effects of migration on Mexican families.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <strong>Rob Fairlie</strong> was interviewed by <i>Fortune</i> magazine, which used his estimates for an article about trends in entrepreneurship in the manufacturing industry.
</p>
<p>
  Ocean scientist <strong>Mary Silver</strong> was featured in a <i>Santa Cruz Sentinel</i> article about research on the oceans' ability to absorb and store excess carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.
</p>
<p>
  In an in-depth story about aging, the <i>New York Times</i> cited research by <strong>Mara Mather</strong> of psychology.
</p>
<p>
  Psychology lecturer <strong>Donald Saposnek</strong> was interviewed by ABC News during the network's coverage of the bitter custody fight over an 8-year-old girl. His comments also appeared on ABC's web site.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Inside Bay Area</i> noted a collaboration between UCSC's <strong>Theater Arts Department</strong> and MusicSources for a Berkeley performance of <i>The Sylvan and Oceanic Delights of Posilipo</i>, citing the participation of "esteemed lutenist, musicologist, and professor at UC Santa Cruz" <strong>Nina Treadwell,</strong> and professor of theater arts <strong>Mark Franko</strong>, "an authority in the court ballet tradition and in reconstructing Renaissance dance."
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Luis Obispo Tribune</i> ran a story about the decline of black abalone, featuring quotes from marine biologist <strong>Peter Raimondi</strong>.
</p>
<p>
  <i>Bridges: A Journal for Jewish Feminists and Our Friends</i> ran a lengthy review of feminist studies professor <strong>Bettina Aptheker</strong>'s recent memoir, <i>Intimate Politics</i>.
</p>

</p>
