<a name="content" id="content"></a>
<p>
  The <i>Nation</i> magazine published an extensive feature article on literature professor <b>Nathaniel Mackey</b>, reviewing his latest book, <i>Splay Anthem</i>, and chronicling his work as a poet, literary critic, fiction writer, and journal editor, which has earned him national and international recognition.
</p>
<p>
  Observations of some of the earliest galaxies to form in the universe by astronomers <b>Rychard Bouwens</b> and <b>Garth Illingworth</b> led to an interview with Illingworth on KGO Radio and stories in numerous media outlets, including the <i>New York Times, International Herald Tribune, Santa Cruz Sentinel</i>, Bay City News Service, BBC News, Voice of America News, Science Now, New Scientist.com, Space Daily, and Space.com.
</p>
<p>
  Economist <b>Lori Kletzer</b>'s proposals to overhaul the unemployment system got great play in the <i>New York Times</i>.
</p>
<p>
  An article in the <i>Contra Costa Times</i> and <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> on climate change in California included quotes from <b>Lisa Sloan</b>, professor of Earth and planetary sciences.
</p>
<p>
  <i>U. S. News &amp; World Report</i> tapped <b>Susanne Jonas</b> of Latin American and Latino studies during its coverage of immigrant activism and how the Latino vote could shape the November elections. She was also interviewed on KPFA Radio's <i>Morning Show</i> about Central American migrants. The discussion focused on the traumas and abuses migrants suffer as they cross Mexico and on deportations by the Mexican government.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>Arizona Republic</i> tapped sociology instructor <b>Mike Males</b> for a story about adult drug deaths. Males noted that little is known about fatal drug use among boomers because the topic has not been studied closely.
</p>
<p>
  An article in <i>New Scientist</i> magazine about the effects of drug trafficking on wildlife conservation described efforts by biology graduate student <b>Hoyt Peckham</b> to save loggerhead turtles in Baja California.
</p>
<p>
  <b>Renee Tajima-Pena</b> of community studies was quoted in an article that appeared in <i>Diverse Issues of Higher Education</i> about the Hurricane Katrina-related loss of a historian's archives documenting the lengthy history of Filipinos on the Gulf Coast.
</p>
<p>
  The <i>San Jose Mercury News</i> enhanced its coverage of the California governor's race by tapping <b>Manuel Pastor</b> of Latin American and Latino studies for insight into Latino voting behavior.
</p>

</p>
